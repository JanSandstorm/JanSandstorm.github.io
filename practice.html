Writing

Literary fiction and poetry in Swedish.

Trygga i Granlyckan (2025) — A woman and her dog encounter a stranger on a forest trail, and a small incident unravels an entire community's quiet history of exclusion and self-deception.

Tid (2025) — Three poems track a single theme across three formal constraints: how time dissolves a childhood city, swallows hours without will, and leaves only scent where a father once was.

What the Painting Wants To Become (2026) — Essay on discovering that defining the outcome in advance is the thing most likely to prevent it.

Tjugonde november (2026,Swedish) — Commandant Horn defends Narva against the tsar's overwhelming army in the autumn of 1700.


Trädgårdsmästaren (work in progress) — A poet discovers that time starts and stops.

Comics

Just like in written literature you can have a narrator, share inner thoughts, and let the reader's imagination work for you (by filling the gutters between panels).

Just like in film you can surprise, show without telling, and plant details that pay off later. Unlike film, you can stage battle scenes and detailed worlds at no extra cost.

Making comics is hard work. So far I have made one: Little Eagle Down (black and white, 2025).


The Void (work in progress): A journalist discovers secrets about the diamond he was given — and about the woman who gave it.

Painting & Drawing

I paint with acrylics and sometimes watercolor. I also draw and sketch — as foundation for both painting and comics.

Music

I am developing my ability to take a musical idea of my own from notation to finished recording. The ambition is instrumental pieces like how Chopin worked, but for guitar.

Reading

I treat reading as a serious part of the practice, not as leisure. Reading like a writer means reading broadly — fiction, non-fiction, good books and bad ones.

I log my books on GoodReads — you're welcome to connect or just check out what I'm currently reading.
